What this is: we take the strategy's actual realised trade returns and re-deal their order 2,000 times (sampling with replacement). Each re-deal is one plausible "alternate history" — the same edge, a different sequence of wins and losses. About this strategy

The idea

This is a trend-following swing strategy on the Nasdaq-100, traded through two 3× leveraged ETFs: TQQQ (3× long) in confirmed uptrends, SQQQ (3× inverse) in confirmed downtrends, and cash whenever neither side fully aligns (~26% of days). It began life as a TradingView Pine strategy (“Ichimoku+++, optimized for TQQQ”) built on an adaptive Ichimoku core plus a large battery of oscillators; that script was interrogated line by line, ported to Python, de-bugged, de-overfit and re-tuned for daily bars — and the deployed configuration keeps only what earned its keep: a simple bull spine, regime-gated exits, and the Ichimoku structure repurposed as the bear-market exit trigger. It stays invested ~92% of days. The toggle at the top shows the same trades leverage-scaled onto the milder QLD/QID (2×) pair.

When it buys TQQQ

Simply: when TQQQ closes back above its 20-day EMA and the VIX curve is not in deep backwardation. That's the whole entry. The original Pine script demanded ~13 additional oscillator blockers — ablation showed they systematically kept the strategy in cash during rebounds (the market's best days), costing roughly 18 points of CAGR. Easy entry, regime-gated grip.

When it buys SQQQ

Only in a real bear tape: bearish price structure below the 20- and 50-day EMAs, the full oversold-blocker set clear, plus two filters added in the Python rework — QQQ below its 200-day SMA (regime gate) and the VIX curve not in steep contango. Bear entries are deliberately rare (17 of 51 backtest trades), sized at half equity, and carry a −10% hard stop, because rallies inside downtrends are violent.

How it exits

Structurally, regime-gated — never on strength. While QQQ holds above its 200-day SMA (bull regime), TQQQ rides through dips, oscillator extremes and even bearish Ichimoku bars; only a VXN crash-spike (99.5th percentile) or a 40% catastrophe trail can force it out. Once QQQ loses the 200-day (bear regime), the Ichimoku structure takes over: a bearish price bar or bearish Chikou closes the position at the next open. The original Pine exited on any of ~17 oscillator triggers — those exits alone cost about two thirds of the compounding. SQQQ exits stay twitchy by design — SuperTrend flip, bullish structure, oversold exhaustion, or the hard stop.

Timing & execution

Signals evaluate on the daily close (confirmed once CBOE posts the day’s EOD prints, ~5:30 PM ET); the resulting order executes at the next market open. No intraday repaint — every decision uses only confirmed daily data, and the backtest fills at the same next open with slippage modeled.

Validation

The configuration was ablation-tested gate by gate, walk-forward split (2011–2019 train 37.9% CAGR → 2019–2026 test 64.9% — the edge is not era-specific), threshold-nudged to confirm it sits on a performance plateau rather than a lucky spike (every ±nudge of the regime length, EMA length, chikou period and stops stays in the 44–52% CAGR band), and stress-tested at 3× the modeled slippage (still ~49.6%). Versus TQQQ buy-and-hold over the same window it delivers roughly 2× the terminal wealth with a ~33-point smaller maximum drawdown. Known Pine bugs (an always-false Williams %R gate, duplicated conditions, dollar-denominated MACD thresholds, an absolute share-count volume filter) were fixed or normalized in the port.

About this dashboard

What this is

An interactive showcase of a personal quantitative research project — a leveraged Nasdaq-100 trend strategy, backtested and presented with a live signal monitor. It exists for demonstration and educational purposes only: to show the strategy’s logic, its backtest, and how the signals are read day to day. It is the companion piece to the VIX Swing Strategy dashboard.

How it was built

Built primarily with Claude Code, on top of the author’s own TradingView Pine research into TQQQ swing systems. The engine is Python; the dashboard is a single self-contained page. More at github.com/markandrewjenkins and the portfolio site.

Data

Live quotes and the volatility readings come from public sources (Yahoo intraday, CBOE end-of-day files, FRED) and are delayed (~5–15 min). Data is provided as-is with no guarantee of accuracy, completeness or availability — feeds can lag or fail. The QLD/QID view is simulated by leverage-scaling the real TQQQ/SQQQ trade returns (calibrated on actual QLD/QID price history).

Not investment advice. Nothing here is a recommendation to buy or sell any security. Backtested and simulated results have inherent limitations, do not represent actual trading, and past performance is not indicative of future results. Leveraged and inverse ETFs decay, compound path-dependently, and can lose most of their value very quickly. Do your own research and consult a licensed professional before trading.
